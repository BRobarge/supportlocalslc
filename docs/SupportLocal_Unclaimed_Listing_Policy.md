# SupportLocalSLC — Unclaimed Listing Policy & Legal Guardrails
*Plain-English guardrails for listing businesses from public data. Keep for yourself; eventually hand to licensees (they'll need the same protections). NOT legal advice — confirm specifics with an attorney before scaling, especially across states.*

---

## THE BOTTOM LINE
Listing factual business information without permission is generally legal and is what every major directory (Yelp, Google, Yellow Pages, TripAdvisor) does. The listing itself is not the risk. The risk is in HOW it's presented. Follow these guardrails and you stay clean.

---

## WHAT'S FINE (do freely)
- Listing a business's factual info without asking: name (as text), address, phone, hours, category, the fact that it exists and what type it is.
- Listing a successful business that doesn't want to be a member. They can be listed anyway, same as on Yelp/Google.
- Making the page look nice and useful.
- Showing your paying businesses in a clearly-labeled "Featured/Sponsored offers" slot on unclaimed pages (comparative/adjacent advertising is normal — Google does it constantly).

## WHAT TO AVOID (the actual risks)
1. **Don't use their IP.** No scraped logos or copyrighted photos from their site/social on unclaimed listings. Use a placeholder or your own/licensed imagery until they claim and upload their own. (Biggest real exposure — handle deliberately.)
2. **Don't imply endorsement or membership.** No fake "Verified"/member badge on a business that hasn't verified. Mark unclaimed listings clearly as unclaimed.
3. **Don't let the page imply the unclaimed business endorses the advertised competitors.** Label the offers slot "Featured local offers" / "Sponsored." Keep it tasteful (2-3).
4. **Don't publish inaccurate info that could harm them** (wrong hours, false "closed"). Keep it accurate.
5. **Don't ignore removal requests.** Provide and honor an easy opt-out.

---

## THE REQUIRED GUARDRAILS (build these in)
1. **Unclaimed imagery rule:** placeholder/owned/licensed only — never scraped logos/photos.
2. **Visible unclaimed status + claim CTA:** "Created from public information. Are you the owner? Claim this listing." (Also your growth mechanic.)
3. **No fake verification:** verified/member badges only for actually-verified members.
4. **Report + removal path:** clear "Report incorrect info" and "Request removal / I'm the owner" on every listing; honor promptly.
5. **Sponsored labeling:** featured/related offers always labeled as featured/sponsored.

---

## THE KEY INSIGHT
The growth mechanic and the legal-safety mechanic are the SAME mechanic. Your cold-start strategy ("claim your existing listing") REQUIRES listings to be honestly marked as unclaimed — which is exactly what keeps you legally clean. Honest presentation = faster growth AND lower risk.

---

## FOR LICENSEES (future SaaS)
When you license the platform, licensees inherit these same obligations. Bake the guardrails into the product defaults (placeholder imagery, unclaimed badges, removal flow, sponsored labels) so licensees are protected by default rather than relying on them to know the rules. This is also a selling point: "compliance-aware by design."
